Changing a Table’s Column Order (Left to Right)
Many screens use a table format to display data. Every table has a standard order in which the columns are listed, from left to right. You can change this order by pinning one or more columns to either the far left or the far right.
EXAMPLE:
The columns in a table might be ordered as follows by default:
Error Text
Source
Target
Error Category
Severity
You could pin Source and then Target to the left, and then pin Error Text to the right, for this result:
Source
Target
Error Category
Severity
Error Text
To pin a column:
1. Identify the column that you want to move to the left or right edge, hover your mouse cursor over the column’s heading to expose the More icon , and then click it.
A context menu opens with a list of options.
2. Choose the Pin to left or Pin to right option, depending on where you want the column to go.
The column is moved to the far left or far right side of the table.
3. (optional) Repeat Step 1 through Step 2 as many times as desired, to move additional columns to the left or right.
To unpin a column so that it is moved back to its original position:
1. Identify the column that you want to unpin, hover your mouse cursor over the column’s heading to expose the More icon , and then click it.
A context menu opens with a list of options.
2. Choose the Unpin option.
The column is moved back to its original position in the table.
In addition, once you log out, the table reverts to its default column order.
